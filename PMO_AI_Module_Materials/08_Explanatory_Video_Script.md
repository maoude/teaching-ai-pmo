# Explanatory Video Script

## Target Length

5 to 10 minutes.

## Video Purpose

Explain the pedagogical design, AI integration, gamification mechanics, and classroom use of the module.

## Suggested Structure

### 0:00-0:45 Introduction

Hello, my project is a gamified AI-assisted pedagogical module titled "From Plan to Control: Managing a Project Like a Real PMO."

It is designed for Licence or Master students in engineering, computer science, business, or management. The module teaches project planning, WBS, scheduling, monitoring, control, risk analysis, and corrective action.

### 0:45-1:45 Course Need and Learning Objectives

The course need is that students often learn project management concepts theoretically, but they do not always experience how these concepts work together in a realistic project situation.

By the end of the module, students can create a WBS, build a schedule, analyze dashboard indicators, diagnose project problems, and propose a professional recovery plan.

### 1:45-3:00 Pedagogical Scenario

The scenario is a Smart Campus Mobile App project. The university wants the app delivered in 12 weeks with a budget of 40,000 USD. The team has developers, a designer, a QA tester, and a project manager. The sponsor expects a first demo by week 6.

Students work in groups as PMO teams. They move from project launch to planning, WBS, scheduling, tracking, crisis management, and final PMO briefing.

### 3:00-4:15 Gamification

The module is gamified through five levels:

1. Project Launch
2. WBS Builder
3. Schedule Rescue
4. Dashboard Detective
5. Crisis Controller

Each level has a quest, points, feedback, and possible badges such as Scope Master, WBS Architect, Schedule Optimizer, Dashboard Detective, Risk Analyst, and Crisis Controller.

### 4:15-5:45 AI Tools

Several AI tools are integrated purposefully.

NotebookLM is used to ground the theoretical content in course notes and reliable sources.

ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude help generate scenarios, instructions, crisis cards, and feedback prompts.

Gamma creates a first version of the slide deck.

Canva improves the visual design and produces worksheets, diagrams, and templates.

Genially supports the branching simulation.

Google Sheets or Excel Online provides the project dashboard and KPI calculations.

Quizizz or QuestionWell supports formative assessment.

### 5:45-7:00 Learning Activities

The module includes different learning types. Students acquire knowledge through short explanations, investigate the project case, practice calculations, collaborate in PMO teams, produce project deliverables, discuss trade-offs, and reflect through an exit ticket.

This variety helps different learner profiles and keeps the session active.

### 7:00-8:30 Demonstration

At this point in the video, show:

1. The Learning Designer module structure.
2. The student worksheet.
3. The gamification levels.
4. The spreadsheet dashboard.
5. The final PMO briefing template.

Explain how students move from one activity to the next.

### 8:30-9:30 Assessment

Assessment includes both formative and summative elements. During the session, students answer polls and quizzes, build a WBS, interpret dashboard data, and make crisis decisions.

At the end, each group submits a mini project plan and delivers a PMO recovery briefing. The rubric evaluates scope clarity, WBS quality, schedule and dependencies, risk analysis, tracking logic, and professional presentation.

### 9:30-10:00 Conclusion

This module uses AI to accelerate preparation and enrich the learning experience, but the instructor remains responsible for validation, guidance, and pedagogical judgment.

The final result is a realistic, interactive, and classroom-ready project management module.
