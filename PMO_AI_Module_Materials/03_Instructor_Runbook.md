# Instructor Runbook

Prepare:

2. Genially branching simulation with the five levels.
3. Google Sheets or Excel workbook with WBS, schedule, budget, risks, tracking, and dashboard tabs.
4. Student worksheet.
5. Quizizz or QuestionWell quiz.
6. PMO briefing template.

## Instructor Script

### Opening

Today you are the PMO team responsible for delivering a Smart Campus Mobile App in 12 weeks. The sponsor expects a demo by week 6, the budget is limited, and the team must handle technical risk. Your job is to manage the project professionally, not simply to finish tasks.

### Transition to Planning

Before any schedule or dashboard can be useful, the team must understand scope, stakeholders, constraints, assumptions, and success criteria.

### Transition to WBS

A WBS is not just a to-do list. It is a structured decomposition of deliverables. If the WBS is weak, the schedule and control system will also be weak.

### Transition to Scheduling

The WBS now becomes activities, dependencies, milestones, and responsibilities. The challenge is that the first schedule is too long, so the PMO must optimize without pretending that work has no limits.

### Transition to Control

A dashboard is useful only when the PM can interpret the numbers and decide what action is needed.

### Crisis Simulation

The project has entered a critical stage. The sponsor refuses an extension, database integration has failed, and the team is overloaded. Your group must propose a recovery plan that is technically realistic and professionally communicated.

## Debrief Questions

1. What was the strongest decision your PMO team made?
2. Which project constraint created the most pressure?
3. What is the difference between monitoring and controlling?
4. What makes a corrective action realistic?
5. How can AI help the PM without replacing professional judgment?
