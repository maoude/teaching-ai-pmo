# Assessment Rubric

## Group Deliverable Rubric

| Criterion | Excellent | Good | Needs Improvement | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | ---: |
| Scope Clarity | Scope, stakeholders, constraints, and success criteria are clear and measurable | Most elements are clear, with minor gaps | Scope is vague or incomplete | 15% |
| WBS Quality | WBS is complete, deliverable-oriented, and logically decomposed | WBS is mostly complete but has some task-list elements | WBS is incomplete or poorly structured | 20% |
| Schedule and Dependencies | Schedule is realistic, optimized, and includes clear dependencies | Schedule is understandable but has some weak assumptions | Schedule is unrealistic or missing dependencies | 20% |
| Risk Analysis | Major risks are identified with relevant mitigation actions | Some risks are identified but mitigation is limited | Risks are missing or generic | 15% |
| Tracking and Control Logic | Dashboard interpretation is accurate and corrective actions are justified | Interpretation is mostly correct with minor calculation or reasoning gaps | Weak interpretation or unclear actions | 20% |
| Professional Presentation | PMO briefing is clear, concise, structured, and convincing | Briefing is understandable but lacks polish or precision | Briefing is unclear or incomplete | 10% |

## Individual Participation

| Criterion | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Preparation | Student understands the case and contributes relevant ideas |
| Collaboration | Student helps the group make decisions |
| Technical Understanding | Student can explain WBS, schedule, dashboard, or control concepts |
| Reflection | Student completes the exit ticket thoughtfully |

## Suggested Grading

Group work: 70%  
Individual participation and reflection: 30%
