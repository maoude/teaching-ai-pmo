# Gamification Design

## Game Frame

Students are not passive learners. They are PMO teams hired by the university to rescue and deliver the Smart Campus Mobile App project.

The module is organized as five levels. Each level has a mission, evidence to produce, points, and feedback.

## Levels and Quests

| Level | Quest | Student Mission | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Level 1 | Project Launch | Define the project scope, stakeholders, constraints, and success criteria | Project charter summary |
| Level 2 | WBS Builder | Build a deliverable-oriented WBS for the Smart Campus App | WBS tree |
| Level 3 | Schedule Rescue | Convert WBS items into a schedule and reduce the plan from 15 weeks to 12 weeks | Optimized schedule |
| Level 4 | Dashboard Detective | Analyze week 6 dashboard data and calculate project health indicators | KPI interpretation |
| Level 5 | Crisis Controller | Respond to the week 8 disaster and present a recovery plan | PMO recovery briefing |

## Points System

| Performance Element | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Clear project scope and stakeholders | 10 |
| Complete and deliverable-oriented WBS | 20 |
| Realistic schedule and dependencies | 20 |
| Correct KPI calculations and interpretation | 20 |
| Risk analysis and corrective actions | 15 |
| Professional PMO briefing | 15 |
| Total | 100 |

## Badges

| Badge | Awarded For |
| --- | --- |
| Scope Master | Defining a clear scope and measurable success criteria |
| WBS Architect | Creating a complete deliverable-oriented WBS |
| Schedule Optimizer | Reducing the schedule without unrealistic assumptions |
| Dashboard Detective | Correctly identifying schedule and cost problems |
| Risk Analyst | Detecting major risks and proposing mitigation actions |
| Crisis Controller | Presenting a convincing recovery strategy |

## Feedback Mechanics

Feedback is provided after every quest:

1. Immediate automated or instructor feedback after polls and quizzes.
2. Peer feedback during group comparison.
3. Dashboard feedback in Google Sheets when values change.
4. Instructor feedback after PMO recovery briefings.

## Rewards

Suggested reward structure:

| Score | Result |
| --- | --- |
| 85-100 | PMO Excellence Award |
| 70-84 | Project Recovery Certified |
| 55-69 | Project Control Apprentice |
| Below 55 | Needs PMO Intervention |

## Branching Decisions

Use these choices in Genially or during live polling.

### Opening Decision

The sponsor announces the project. What should the PM do first?

A. Start coding immediately  
B. Define scope and stakeholders  
C. Buy software tools  
D. Ask for more budget  

Correct answer: B.

### Schedule Decision

The first plan takes 15 weeks, but the deadline is 12 weeks. What should the PMO do?

A. Hide the problem  
B. Compress the schedule using parallel work and scope prioritization  
C. Remove testing  
D. Add features to impress the sponsor  

Best answer: B.

### Crisis Decision

At week 8, integration fails and the team is overloaded. What is the best recovery action?

A. Add more features  
B. Freeze scope and focus on login, schedule, and notifications  
C. Stop testing  
D. Delay communication until week 12  

Best answer: B.
