# Explanatory Video Script

## Target Length

8 to 10 minutes

## Video Title

Gamified AI-Assisted Project Management Module: From Plan to Control

## 0:00-0:45 Introduction

Hello. This project is a gamified AI-assisted pedagogical module titled "From Plan to Control: Managing a Project Like a Real PMO."

It is designed for Licence or Master students in engineering, computer science, business, or management. The lecture teaches project planning, WBS, scheduling, monitoring, control, risk analysis, and corrective action.

## 0:45-1:45 Course Need

Students often learn project management as separate concepts: charter, WBS, schedule, risk, dashboard, and control. The difficulty is that they do not always experience how these elements connect inside a realistic project.

This module solves that problem by placing students inside a project scenario. They act as a PMO team responsible for delivering a Smart Campus Mobile App in 12 weeks.

## 1:45-2:45 Learning Objectives

By the end of the module, students can build a deliverable-oriented WBS, transform the WBS into a schedule, optimize execution under constraints, interpret dashboard indicators, diagnose project health, and present a corrective action plan.

These objectives cover knowledge, comprehension, application, production, collaboration, and reflection.

## 2:45-4:00 Scenario

The case is a Smart Campus Mobile App. The university wants student login, course schedule, exam calendar, notifications, room reservation, complaint/request system, admin dashboard, and pilot deployment.

The constraints are a 40,000 USD budget, a 12-week deadline, a small team, a first demo by week 6, and a major risk related to database integration.

The scenario is realistic because students understand university digital services and can quickly imagine the stakeholders and risks.

## 4:00-5:15 Gamification

The module is structured as five levels.

Level 1 is Project Launch. Students define scope and stakeholders and earn the Scope Master badge.

Level 2 is WBS Builder. Students create a deliverable-oriented WBS and earn the WBS Architect badge.

Level 3 is Schedule Rescue. Students compress a 15-week plan into 12 weeks and earn the Schedule Optimizer badge.

Level 4 is Dashboard Detective. Students analyze project data and earn the Dashboard Detective badge.

Level 5 is Crisis Controller. Students respond to a week 8 project crisis and earn the Risk Analyst and Crisis Controller badges.

## 5:15-6:45 AI Tools

NotebookLM is used to ground the theoretical content in uploaded course sources.

ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude help generate scenarios, prompts, crisis cards, rubrics, and feedback.

Learning Designer structures the module and provides the required export and public link.

Gamma and Canva support slide creation and visual design.

Genially supports the branching crisis simulation.

Google Sheets or Excel supports the schedule and KPI dashboard simulation.

Quizizz or QuestionWell provides formative quizzes and instant feedback.

## 6:45-8:00 Demonstration

In the student-facing page, students can read the syllabus, understand the scenario, see the five quests, download the dashboard CSV, and review the assessment rubric.

In class, the instructor uses the page as the central hub, while students complete the worksheet and group tasks.

The dashboard CSV can be opened in Google Sheets or Excel so students can analyze the week 6 project status.

## 8:00-9:15 Assessment

Assessment is both formative and summative.

During the lecture, students answer polls, complete quizzes, build the WBS, optimize the schedule, and diagnose the dashboard.

At the end, each group presents a three-minute PMO recovery briefing. The rubric evaluates scope clarity, WBS quality, schedule and dependencies, risk analysis, tracking and control logic, and professional presentation.

## 9:15-10:00 Conclusion

This module uses AI to accelerate preparation and increase interaction, but the instructor remains responsible for judgment, correction, and pedagogical guidance.

The final result is a realistic, transferable, classroom-ready learning module aligned with the AUF final project requirements.
