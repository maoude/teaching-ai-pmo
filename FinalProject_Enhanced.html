AI Integration in Higher Education — AUF Workshop
🎓 University Course Design 🤖 Multiple AI Tools 🎮 Gamification Mechanics 📐 Learning Designer
📌 The Task

Design and gamify a university disciplinary course module that integrates multiple AI tools and diverse learning types to strengthen student engagement, autonomy, and learning.

The module must be realistic, pedagogically coherent, and directly transferable to a real classroom. You are expected to go beyond simply adding AI as a gimmick — every tool and mechanic must serve a clear pedagogical purpose.

🎯 Project Objectives
1
Analyze & Define

Analyze your course and formulate clear, measurable learning objectives aligned with the module content. Use Bloom's Taxonomy to guide verb choice (understand, apply, evaluate, create…).

2
Design the Scenario

Conceive a complete pedagogical scenario integrating gamification mechanics (levels, quests, progression, feedback, rewards) and multiple AI tools. Ensure mechanics reinforce — not distract from — the learning goals.

3
Build the Module

Develop a functional prototype using your chosen AI tools — ready for classroom use, covering varied learning types (knowledge, comprehension, application, creation…).

4
Present & Iterate

Present the project to peers, gather structured feedback, and refine accordingly. Be prepared to justify every pedagogical and design choice.

📊 Evaluation Grid
Pedagogical Relevance
Objectives are clear, measurable, and address a genuine course need. Activities are logically structured. 💡 Use action verbs from Bloom's Taxonomy. Each activity should trace back to a stated objective.
20%
AI & Gamification Integration
Multiple AI tools used purposefully (≥2–3 tools). Gamification mechanics (levels, quests, badges, feedback…) meaningfully integrated and supporting learning objectives. 💡 Each AI tool should serve a distinct role. Gamification must be pedagogically justified, not decorative.
30%
Learning Variety
Module covers diverse learning types: knowledge acquisition, practice/quiz, creation, collaboration, reflection. Addresses different learner profiles. 💡 Aim for at least 4 different activity types. Include both individual and collaborative tasks.
20%
Scenario Quality
The pedagogical scenario is clear, progressive, and well-aligned. Activities build toward the stated outcomes. 💡 Activities should escalate in complexity. A student should feel clear progression through the module.
15%
Feasibility & Presentation
The module is classroom-ready. The explanatory video clearly communicates design choices and demonstrates tools in use. 💡 Could a substitute teacher run your module without extra guidance? That's the feasibility bar.
15%
🧠 Bloom's Taxonomy — Writing Measurable Objectives

Every learning objective must use a measurable action verb. Pick verbs from the appropriate cognitive level for your module's goals.

Level 1
Remember
list · recall · name · identify · define · recognize
Level 2
Understand
explain · summarize · describe · classify · interpret · paraphrase
Level 3
Apply
use · solve · demonstrate · calculate · execute · implement
Level 4
Analyze
compare · differentiate · examine · break down · critique · distinguish
Level 5
Evaluate
assess · judge · justify · argue · defend · prioritize
Level 6
Create
design · build · produce · develop · formulate · construct
✏️
Objective formula: "By the end of this module, students will be able to [verb from Bloom's] + [content] + [condition or standard]." Example: "Students will be able to design a project risk matrix using an AI brainstorming tool, covering at least 5 identified risks."

🎮 Gamification Mechanics — Reference

Choose mechanics that reinforce learning behaviors — exploration, persistence, reflection — not just reward completion. Each mechanic must map to a learning goal.

🗺️
Quests / Missions
Structured challenges with clear goals and deliverables at each stage
⬆️
Levels / Progression
Scaffold complexity — unlock higher content by mastering the basics first
🏅
Badges / Achievements
Recognize specific skills or behaviors beyond grades and marks
⏱️
Time Challenges
Timed tasks to build fluency, retrieval practice, and quick decision-making
💬
Instant Feedback
Immediate, specific feedback after each activity — AI can personalize this
🏆
Leaderboard
Optional — use carefully; can demotivate lower performers if poorly designed
🎲
Random Events
Surprise bonuses or challenge twists to maintain curiosity and engagement
🤝
Team Roles
Assign interdependent roles to drive collaboration and shared accountability
📅 Suggested Work Timeline
Phase 1
Analysis & Objective Setting
Select your course/module. Write 3–5 measurable learning objectives using Bloom's action verbs. Identify the learner profile and context constraints (time, tech access, class size).
Phase 2
Scenario & Gamification Design
Draft the full pedagogical scenario. Map activities to objectives. Choose and justify gamification mechanics. Select AI tools and define their specific pedagogical roles.
Phase 3
Build in Learning Designer
Create the module in Learning Designer. Ensure activity variety (at least 4 types). Test each AI tool integration. Export and publish with a public link — test in incognito mode.
Phase 4
Record the Explanatory Video
Record a 5–10 min screen-captured walkthrough. Narrate your design rationale. Show AI tools and gamification mechanics live. Upload to a public platform (YouTube, Google Drive).
Phase 5
Present, Peer Review & Refine
Present to peers. Collect structured feedback using the peer review guide below. Revise the module and video if needed. Confirm all three deliverables have public links before final submission.
🔍 Peer Review Guide

Use these structured questions when reviewing a peer's module. Give specific, constructive feedback — not just ratings.

As a Learner — Could you follow this module?
  1. Are the objectives clear enough to know what you'll be able to do by the end?
  2. Does each activity feel purposeful, or does any feel like filler?
  3. Is the difficulty progression logical — does it build on what came before?
  4. Do the gamification elements motivate you, or do they feel disconnected?
  5. Is anything confusing, missing, or assumed without explanation?
As an Instructor — Could you deliver this module?
  1. Are the AI tools clearly explained — could another instructor use them?
  2. Is the time estimate realistic for each activity?
  3. Are the gamification mechanics manageable in a real classroom?
  4. Does the module address different learner profiles and needs?
  5. What is the single most important improvement you would suggest?
✅ Pre-Submission Checklist
  • Learning Designer module exported and published under CC-BY-NC (or equivalent open license)
  • Public Learning Designer link tested in incognito/private browser — no login required
  • Explanatory video is 5–10 minutes, publicly accessible (YouTube unlisted, Google Drive public share, etc.)
  • Module includes at least 2–3 clearly distinct AI tools, each with a defined pedagogical role
  • At least 4 different learning activity types are present (acquisition, practice, creation, collaboration…)
  • All learning objectives use measurable Bloom's action verbs
  • Gamification mechanics are justified in the video — not just cosmetic additions
  • Activities build progressively in complexity toward the stated outcomes
  • The module could be run by another instructor without additional guidance
  • The video narrates design rationale, not just a screen tour
  • Peer feedback has been collected and at least one revision has been made based on it
