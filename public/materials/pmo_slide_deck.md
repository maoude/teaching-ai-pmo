# PMO Lecture Slide Deck

## Slide 1: From Plan to Control

Managing a project like a real PMO.

- Case: Smart Campus Mobile App
- Duration: 3 hours
- Mode: gamified AI-assisted lecture

## Slide 2: Today You Are the PMO

Your university needs a Smart Campus Mobile App in 12 weeks.

- Budget: 40,000 USD
- Demo required by week 6
- Main risk: database integration

## Slide 3: Learning Goals

By the end, students can move from planning to project control.

- Build a deliverable-oriented WBS
- Create and optimize a schedule
- Read dashboard indicators
- Propose corrective action

## Slide 4: Project Scope

The Smart Campus App has several visible deliverables.

- Student login
- Course schedule and exam calendar
- Notifications and room reservation
- Complaint system and admin dashboard

## Slide 5: Level 1 - Project Launch

A PM starts by clarifying the project foundation.

- Identify stakeholders
- Define success criteria
- State constraints and assumptions
- Earn: Scope Master

## Slide 6: Level 2 - WBS Builder

The WBS decomposes deliverables, not random tasks.

- Project management
- Requirements
- Design
- Development
- Testing
- Deployment

## Slide 7: Level 3 - Schedule Rescue

The first plan takes 15 weeks. The sponsor requires 12.

- Parallelize UI and backend work
- Prioritize critical scope
- Delay advanced features
- Avoid removing testing

## Slide 8: Level 4 - Dashboard Detective

Week 6 data shows the project is in trouble.

- Planned progress: 50%; actual: 38%
- Planned cost: 20,000 USD; actual: 24,000 USD
- Critical bugs: 12
- Completed modules: 2 instead of 4

## Slide 9: Level 5 - Crisis Controller

Week 8: database integration fails and the team is overloaded.

- Freeze non-critical scope
- Focus login, schedule, notifications
- Communicate impact
- Ask sponsor for a decision

## Slide 10: Final PMO Briefing

Every group presents a three-minute recovery briefing.

- What happened?
- What is the impact?
- What corrective action is proposed?
- What decision is needed?
