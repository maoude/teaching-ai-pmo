Explanatory Video Script

Target length: 5 to 10 minutes

0:00-0:45 Introduction

Hello, my project is a gamified AI-assisted pedagogical module titled "From Plan to Control: Managing a Project Like a Real PMO." It is designed for Licence or Master students in engineering, computer science, business, or management.

0:45-1:45 Course Need and Learning Objectives

The course need is that students often learn project management concepts theoretically, but they do not always experience how these concepts work together in a realistic project situation. By the end of the module, students can create a WBS, build a schedule, analyze dashboard indicators, diagnose project problems, and propose a professional recovery plan.

1:45-3:00 Pedagogical Scenario

The scenario is a Smart Campus Mobile App project. The university wants the app delivered in 12 weeks with a budget of 40,000 USD. Students work as PMO teams and move from project launch to planning, WBS, scheduling, tracking, crisis management, and final briefing.

3:00-4:15 Gamification

The module is gamified through five levels: Project Launch, WBS Builder, Schedule Rescue, Dashboard Detective, and Crisis Controller. Each level has a quest, points, feedback, and badges such as Scope Master, WBS Architect, Schedule Optimizer, Dashboard Detective, Risk Analyst, and Crisis Controller.

4:15-5:45 AI Tools

NotebookLM grounds the theory in course sources. ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude generate scenarios, instructions, and crisis cards. Gamma creates a first slide deck. Canva improves visuals and worksheets. Genially supports the branching simulation. Google Sheets or Excel provides the dashboard. Quizizz or QuestionWell supports formative assessment.

5:45-7:00 Learning Activities

The module includes acquisition, inquiry, practice, production, collaboration, discussion, and reflection. Students do not only listen; they produce project management artifacts and make decisions under constraints.

7:00-8:30 Demonstration

Show the Learning Designer module structure, student worksheet, gamification levels, spreadsheet dashboard, and final PMO briefing template. Explain how students move from one activity to the next.

8:30-9:30 Assessment

Assessment includes polls, quizzes, WBS production, dashboard interpretation, crisis decisions, and a final PMO briefing. The rubric evaluates scope clarity, WBS quality, schedule and dependencies, risk analysis, tracking logic, and professional presentation.

9:30-10:00 Conclusion

This module uses AI to accelerate preparation and enrich the learning experience, but the instructor remains responsible for validation, guidance, and pedagogical judgment. The final result is a realistic, interactive, and classroom-ready project management module.
